Offensive Line Drills

Everything on offense starts with the line. If you can’t block, you can’t move the ball. I teach my offensive linemen that they are the engine of the team — the unit that makes everything else possible. It’s not the flashiest job, but it’s one of the most important. We train fundamentals, toughness, and unity up front.

Stance

Teaching a proper three-point stance and explosive first step. Focus on posture, balance, and firing out low.

  1. Feet
  2. Slide
  3. Squeeze
  4. Sink
  5. Down

Wide Zone Footwork Drill

Wide zone footwork is a blocking technique used in the wide zone running scheme. It focuses on lateral movement and maintaining leverage against defenders.

  1. From a 3-point stance
  2. Line up on the left or right side of a line behind the hash.
  3. Explode out of stance with first step being up and over the horizontal and vertical line.

Inside Zone Footwork Drill

Inside zone footwork is a blocking technique used in the inside zone running scheme. It focuses on driving defenders off the line of scrimmage and creating running lanes.

  1. From a 3-point stance
  2. Line up on the left or right side of a line with back foot in front of hash.
  3. Start by stepping over the line with the outside foot.
  4. Power step forward.
  5. Drive forward in a wide stance with short choppy steps.

Pass Set Drill

Pass set footwork is a blocking technique used in pass protection. It focuses on maintaining balance and leverage against defenders while protecting the quarterback.

  1. Back off the ball
  2. Get in two-point stance
  3. Kick back 3 times with the outside foot.
  4. Fire Feet

Down Block

Down blocking is a technique used to block defenders who are aligned inside the offensive lineman. It focuses on driving the defender off the line of scrimmage and creating running lanes.

Reach Block

Reach blocking is a technique used to block defenders who are aligned outside the offensive lineman. It focuses on getting the offensive lineman's body in front of the defender and driving them off the line of scrimmage.
