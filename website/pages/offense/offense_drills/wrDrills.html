Route Tree

In most offensive formations, receivers are identified as X, Y, and Z:

  • X Receiver: Lines up on the line of scrimmage, usually outside.
  • Z Receiver: Lines up off the line of scrimmage, often used in motion.
  • Y Receiver: Typically the tight end, aligned near the offensive line.

Below are fundamental routes every young receiver should know. These are the foundation for building more advanced concepts.

Slant

Setup: Start on either side of the QB. Confirm the direction of the slant pre-snap.

Directions: Run forward a few yards, then cut at a 45-degree angle.

Tips: Use a quick juke before your cut to create separation from the defender.

Comeback (Hitch)

Setup: Determine depth with the QB—can be short or deep.

Directions: Run to your depth, plant, and come back 1–2 yards to the QB.

Tips: Be ready to pivot upfield after the catch.

Flat / 5-Yard Out

Setup: Line up wide, 5+ yards from the sideline.

Directions: Run straight 5 yards, then break 90 degrees toward the sideline.

Tips: This is a quick route to exploit soft spots in the defense.

Post

Setup: Start outside. Leave space between you and the QB to cut inside.

Directions: Run 5–10 yards, then cut at a 45-degree angle toward the center.

Tips: Think of cutting toward the goalpost—hence the name "post."

Corner

Setup: Similar to the post route but aiming for the outside corner.

Directions: Run 5–10 yards, then cut at a 45-degree angle away from the field center.

Tips: Plant hard on your inside foot to make a sharp, effective cut.

Fade

Setup: Start with some inside spacing. Prepare to break outside quickly.

Directions: Step inward at the snap, then accelerate toward the deep sideline.

Tips: Stay low for better jukes. Use your body to shield the defender.

Go / Fly

Setup: Start anywhere on the line. Be ready to sprint off the snap.

Directions: Run straight at full speed downfield.

Tips: Time your glance over the shoulder for the ball to avoid slowing down too early.
